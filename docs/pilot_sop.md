# PDD App Pilot - Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

**Role:** Station Clerk / Pilot User
**Objective:** Record precise event times to identify Pre-Departure Detention (PDD) accurately.

---

## ‚úÖ WHAT YOU MUST DO

1.  **Start a New Session:**
    - Enter the correct **Train Number**.
    - Select **Train Type** (Mail/Express, etc.) and **Loco Type**.
    - Verify the **Scheduled Departure (STD)** matches the working timetable.

2.  **Record Events as they Identify:**
    - Tap the event button **IMMEDIATELY** when it happens.
    - Example: Tap "Loco Attached" when you see the loco coupling is complete.
    - Example: Tap "Ready To Depart" when the signal is given and everything is ready.

3.  **Check Status:**
    - **Green (On Time):** Good job.
    - **Orange (At Risk):** STD is crossed, but not ready yet. Hurry up!
    - **Red (Delayed):** Train is detained.

4.  **End of Day:**
    - Go to **Reports** -> **Daily Summary**.
    - Verify the counts match your manual register (if any).
    - Inform Supervisor if "Supervisor Tools" export is needed.

---

## üö´ WHAT YOU MUST NOT DO

1.  **DO NOT** guess times. Only record what you see or hear confirmed on walkie-talkie.
2.  **DO NOT** leave the app open in background for hours. Best to open it 30 mins before train arrival.
3.  **DO NOT** try to correct past events yourself. If you made a mistake, tell the Supervisor. They have a special correction tool.
